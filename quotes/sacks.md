# Rabbi Jonathan Sacks — Curated Quotes

*Selected primarily from The Great Partnership: Science, Religion and the Search for Meaning (2011)*
*Annotated with my own thinking*

---

## On Science and Religion as Complements

> *"Science takes things apart to see how they work. Religion puts things together to see what they mean."*
> — The Great Partnership

**My reading:** This is the cleanest formulation of the distinction I've encountered. Both are necessary. A civilisation with only science knows mechanism without meaning. A civilisation with only religion knows meaning without mechanism. The richest human life holds both — and knows which question to ask when.

---

## On the Two Brains of Civilisation

> *"Athens and Jerusalem — science and scripture — are the two foundations of Western civilisation, and they are more complementary than they are at odds."*
> — The Great Partnership (paraphrase)

**My reading:** The Enlightenment set these against each other. Sacks restores the partnership. I believe he is right. The most rigorous scientific thinkers I admire (including the founders of quantum mechanics) were almost universally people of deep spiritual conviction. Coincidence? I don't think so.

---

## On Meaning and Purpose

> *"The question 'Why?' cannot be answered by any physical science, because it is not a question about physics. It is a question about meaning, and science does not deal in meanings, only in facts."*
> — The Great Partnership

**My reading:** This is why the purely materialist worldview ultimately fails. It can describe the universe in extraordinary detail but cannot tell you why it matters that you are in it. Torah answers that question. B'tzelem Elohim is the answer: you are in it because you were placed here, and your presence carries weight.

---

## On Human Dignity

> *"To be human is to be the one life-form known to us in the universe that asks the question 'Why?'"*
> — The Great Partnership (paraphrase)

**My reading:** This is one of the deepest distinctions between humans and every other form of life. We are the meaning-seeking animal. Any economic or political system that ignores this — that treats people purely as production units or consumption nodes — is in fundamental conflict with what humans actually are.

---

## On the Abrahamic Revolution

> *"The monotheistic revolution was the discovery that the universe is not a battleground of gods, each pursuing their own interests, but a single creation reflecting a single will — and therefore intelligible, and therefore subject to moral law."*
> — The Great Partnership (paraphrase)

**My reading:** This is why the prohibition on dishonest measures is so deeply embedded in Torah. An arbitrary universe, run by competing gods, has no basis for universal justice. A created universe, reflecting a single rational and moral creator, demands that the same rules apply to everyone — including the powerful.

---

## On Hope

> *"Optimism is the belief that things will get better. Hope is the belief that, together, we can make things better."*
> — Various speeches

**My reading:** I am not an optimist about fiat money, about the trajectory of many Western institutions, or about the political short-termism I observe. But I am a person of hope — because I believe that the tools now exist (Bitcoin, decentralised systems, open knowledge) for people to build better institutions without waiting for the existing ones to reform themselves.
