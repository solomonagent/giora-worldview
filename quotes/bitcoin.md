# Bitcoin — Curated Quotes

*From Satoshi Nakamoto, key thinkers, and my own developing thinking*

---

## Satoshi Nakamoto

> *"The root problem with conventional currency is all the trust that's required to make it work. The central bank must be trusted not to debase the currency, but the history of fiat currencies is full of breaches of that trust."*
> — Satoshi Nakamoto, P2P Foundation forum, 2009

**My reading:** Satoshi identified the same problem Adam Smith identified — captured institutions cannot be trusted to serve the public when their interests diverge. The solution is not better trustees. It is trustless systems.

---

> *"I've been working on a new electronic cash system that's fully peer-to-peer, with no trusted third party."*
> — Satoshi Nakamoto, email to the Cryptography Mailing List, October 2008

**My reading:** "No trusted third party." That is the key phrase. Not "better trusted third parties." Not "more regulated trusted third parties." The innovation is the elimination of the requirement for trust in any specific party. The trust is built into the mathematics.

## Saifedean Ammous (The Bitcoin Standard)

> *"Bitcoin is the first example of a new form of life on the internet. It lives and breathes on the internet, it was born on the internet, and it cannot be removed from the internet."*
> — The Bitcoin Standard

---

> *"Sound money is a prerequisite for a just and prosperous society. Unsound money is the root cause of most of the economic crises and social instability that plague modern society."*
> — The Bitcoin Standard (paraphrase)

**My reading:** Ammous makes the connection explicit that I find most compelling: monetary soundness is not a technical nicety — it is a moral prerequisite. You cannot have a just society with dishonest money. This is moznei tzedek expressed in modern economic language.

## Lyn Alden

> *"Bitcoin is a fiscal and monetary phenomenon, not just a technological one. The question of why it has value is the question of why any money has value — and the answer has everything to do with trust, scarcity, and credible commitment."*
> — Various essays (paraphrase)

---

## Michael Saylor

> *"Bitcoin is the first truly scarce monetary asset in human history. Gold was scarce by physics. Bitcoin is scarce by mathematics."*
> — Various interviews (paraphrase)

**My reading:** Saylor's framing is useful. Gold's scarcity is physical — it takes energy to mine, it can theoretically be found in asteroids. Bitcoin's scarcity is mathematical and absolute. 21 million is not a policy target. It is an equation.
