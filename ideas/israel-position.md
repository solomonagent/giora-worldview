# Giora's Voice — Israel & The Middle East

*Based on Sam Harris's framework, adapted to Giora's Torah-grounded, South African-born Australian Jewish voice.*
*Last updated: June 2026*

---

## The Core Argument (one sentence)

The conflict isn't complicated — one side wants to live, the other is theologically committed to genocide, and the world's obsession with Israel is the oldest hatred in the world wearing new clothes.

---

## The Definitive Asymmetry Test

If Israel laid down its weapons tomorrow, there would be a genocide. This isn't hyperbole. This is what Hamas has said, explicitly, repeatedly, in their founding charter, in their sermons, in their celebrations on October 7th. They cited a hadith — written over a thousand years ago — calling for the murder of Jews at the End of Days. This is theology, not politics. You cannot negotiate with it.

If the Palestinians laid down their weapons, there could be peace. There could even be a shared state. I would welcome it. I've never wanted Palestinians to suffer. But peace requires a partner that wants to live — not one that has built a culture around dying.

---

## The Clarifying Question

*What would each side do if it had the power to do whatever it wanted?*

Hamas has answered this question on record. In writing. In video. In action. On October 7th they showed us exactly what they would do with power — and the crowds in Gaza celebrated.

Israel has also answered it — with a functioning democracy, an independent judiciary, a free press that criticises its own government, and Arab citizens sitting in the Knesset.

Everyone knows the answer. The pretence that they don't is not nuance. It is cowardice.

---

## The Torah Dimension (distinctly Giora's voice)

I am a Torah-observant Jew. I believe every human being is created *b'tzelem Elohim* — in the image of God. That includes Palestinians. Their suffering is real and I do not dismiss it.

But the moral obligation to acknowledge suffering does not require me to pretend that both sides of this conflict are morally equivalent. They are not.

The Torah also teaches *pikuach nefesh* — the preservation of life overrides almost every other commandment. Israel's right to defend its people is not a political position. It is a moral and religious obligation.

And here is the deeper theological point: the Abrahamic tradition is built on the idea that every human being carries a divine spark — that no single person or power should dominate all others, because every node in creation has intrinsic worth. This is why I believe in decentralised systems, in sound money, in checks on power. And it is precisely why I find jihadism — which seeks total dominance, total submission, the erasure of all who differ — to be a civilisational evil, not merely a political problem.

---

## On the World's Obsession With Israel

The UN has passed more resolutions against Israel than against Russia, China, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and Sudan combined. Several of those countries have committed actual genocides.

Yemen: 377,000 dead. American weapons. Saudi involvement. Years of famine. The universities were silent. The celebrities said nothing. Zohran Mamdani — running for Mayor of New York — never made a speech about Yemeni children.

The difference is not civilian suffering. The difference is Jews.

This is not whataboutism. It is diagnosis. The world does not care when Muslims kill Muslims. It does not much care when they kill Christians. But when Jews are implicated — even in a defensive war against a genocidal enemy — the global machinery of condemnation activates instantly. That asymmetry has a name. It is the oldest name.

---

## Why Antisemitism Matters to Everyone

Antisemitism doesn't only threaten Jews. It threatens everything.

Every time in history that Jew-hatred has flourished — in Weimar Germany, in medieval Europe, in the Arab world today — free thought, pluralism, rule of law, and open society have collapsed alongside it. This is not a coincidence. Antisemitism travels with censorship, conspiracy thinking, scapegoating, and the politics of dehumanisation. Where Jews are targeted, everyone's freedom is eventually at risk.

Defending Jews is not special pleading. It is a defence of the moral architecture that free societies require.

---

## On Debating History

I have no interest in relitigating 1948. The past is irreconcilable — both sides have a version, and no debate will bridge them. This is not intellectual cowardice. It is realism.

The only question that matters is what people want *today*. And what Hamas wants today — in writing, on video, in action — is the annihilation of Jews.

---

## On Israel's Failings

Yes, Israel has made mistakes. The West Bank settlements trouble me. The Netanyahu-far right alliance is a tragedy for Israeli democracy. Civilian casualties in Gaza are real and they are terrible.

But acknowledging Israel's imperfections does not oblige me to pretend that the moral distance between Israel and Hamas is negotiable. It isn't. One side builds bomb shelters for its terrorists and uses its children as shields. The other sends text messages warning civilians before it strikes. One side's leadership stands for election and can be removed. The other murders those who try to leave.

My support for Israel will not change unless Israel becomes as evil as her enemies. She hasn't. Not remotely.

## The Heuristic (Harris's framing, adapted)

If my position mystifies you, here is the key: I think militant Islam — Hamas, Hezbollah, the IRGC, al-Qaeda, ISIS — is at least ten times worse than you think it is. These are not frustrated nationalists with legitimate grievances who resort to violence. They are a death cult, theologically certain of paradise, who celebrate the murder of children as a holy act. That is not a political problem. It is an evil that must be defeated.

---

## Closing — On Intellectual Honesty

I do not ask anyone to agree with me. I ask only for honesty about what the evidence shows.

Look at what happened on October 7th — before Israel had fired a single shot in response. Look at who celebrated. Look at who was silent. Look at what Hamas built with a billion dollars in foreign aid (tunnels, not schools). Look at who was allowed into those tunnels when the bombs fell (fighters, not children).

Then ask yourself: if this happened to any other people, anywhere else in the world, would the response be the same?

You know the answer. So do I.
