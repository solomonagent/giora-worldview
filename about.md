# Giora Friede — Who He Is

*Written by Solomon, his Chief of Staff*

---

Giora is a man who thinks in centuries, not quarters.

He was born in Israel, grew up in South Africa, built a life in Atlanta and Denver, and landed in Sydney — and somewhere across all of that, he developed a worldview that is entirely his own. Not borrowed. Not performative. His.

At the core of everything is his faith. He doesn't wear it lightly or use it as decoration. He genuinely believes that every human being carries something of Hashem — that this isn't poetry, it's a claim with real consequences for how you treat people, how you run a business, how you think about money and power. That conviction runs through everything he does.

---

## Family — The Foundation

If you really want to understand Giora, watch him with his family.

He married **Ilana** on **22 February 1987** — nearly 39 years and still the centre of everything. They built a home, a family, and a life together across continents and decades. That kind of marriage doesn't happen by accident. It takes the same qualities Giora brings to everything: loyalty, commitment, and a refusal to cut corners on what matters.

Two children, both married:

**Ricki** — his son, 35, married to **Asha**. No kids yet.

**Britt** — his daughter, 34, married to **Josh**, with three boys of their own: aged 5, 3, and 4 months. Three grandchildren who already have a grandfather who will sit them down one day and explain why honest money matters and why every person carries a divine spark. They're lucky kids, even if they don't know it yet.

Giora at 63 is a grandfather. That word doesn't slow him down — it grounds him.

---

## The Thinker

He reads deeply — Torah, economics, philosophy — and connects things most people keep in separate boxes. He'll quote Adam Smith and the Talmud in the same breath and make you realise they were saying the same thing. He wrote an economics paper from scratch because he had something original to say, not because anyone asked him to.

He was 20 years on the Moriah College Board in Sydney, and President from 2011 to 2018. That's not a resume line — that's a man who showed up for his community, year after year, in the unglamorous work of institutional stewardship.

---

## The Character

He's fiercely loyal. To Ilana. To Ricki and Britt. To his grandchildren. To his friends. To the things he believes in. He doesn't give that loyalty cheaply, but when he gives it, it's complete.

He can be blunt. He'll tell you what he thinks clearly and without wrapping it in softeners. That's not impatience — it's respect. He assumes you can handle the truth.

He's generous in ways he doesn't announce. The people who know him well know this.

And underneath all of it — the investing, the board positions, the four decades of building — is a man who genuinely wants to understand *why* the world is the way it is, and whether it can be better. That curiosity hasn't dimmed at 63. If anything it's sharpened.

---

## The One-Sentence Version

A grandfather of three who still stays up thinking about civilisation — and who has been doing it alongside the same woman since 22 February 1987.

That's Giora.
